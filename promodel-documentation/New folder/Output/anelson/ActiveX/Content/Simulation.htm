Simulation

Simulation is the act of running your model through Process Simulator's simulation engine. During simulation, your entities flow through your process according to their routings. Numerical values are also assigned to your statistical distributions. You may simulate your model at any time during its development to troubleshoot problems and verify that it is being developed properly.

Before you Simulate

Prior to Simulation you should define Simulation Options (see Simulation Options on page 1), and have placed at least one entity, arrival connection, and activity on the Visio layout.

Starting the Simulation

You may start the simulation by:

  • Selecting Simulate from the Process Simulator ribbon.
  • Right-clicking on a blank portion of the Visio layout and choosing Simulate from the context menu.
  • Opening the Scenario Manager from the Process Simulator ribbon, and selecting Run Scenarios. (Note: this will begin simulating the scenarios marked for simulation in the Scenario Manager.)

As the simulation begins, your process will be compiled and translated. During this time, the model will be checked for errors and common mistakes. If errors are found, you will be notified and you will have to correct the issues before simulation is possible.

Simulation Ribbon During Run-time

Once compilation and translation of the process into a simulation model is complete, the layout view will change to reflect the run-time environment. During simulation you will have several Run-Time Options (see Run-Time Options on page 1).

Completion of the Simulation

Upon completion of the simulation, you will be prompted whether you would like to view simulation results. Click Yes to open the Output Viewer. For more information, see Viewing Output Results (see Viewing Output Results on page 1).
